{{< framework
  name="The Two Engine Framework"
  tagline="Stock prices are driven by two engines — EPS and PE. Multi-baggers need both."
  explanation="A stock's return decomposes into two engines: the EPS engine (fundamental earnings growth) and the PE engine (multiple expansion driven by sentiment, moat strength, and margin trajectory). Linear returns come when only EPS expands. Exponential returns come when both engines fire together. The same company can make one investor 4x and lose another money over a decade — the difference is whether the PE engine still has runway at entry."
  relevance="Position sizing, entry timing, distinguishing 'good business at a fair price' from 'good business at a finished price.'"
