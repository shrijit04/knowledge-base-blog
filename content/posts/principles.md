A running list of ideas I find myself returning to — collected here so I can grow it over time.

---

{{< principle
  name="Asymmetric Loss"
  tagline="Not knowing is better than knowing wrong."
  explanation="When the downside of false confidence outweighs the downside of uncertainty, staying ignorant is the safer position. A wrong map actively misleads you; no map at least keeps you alert. The danger isn't the gap in your knowledge — it's the illusion that the gap doesn't exist."
  relevance="Risk assessment, investing, medical diagnosis, any high-stakes decision where acting on bad information is worse than pausing."
>}}

{{< principle
  name="Critical Mass"
  tagline="Nothing happens — until suddenly everything happens."
  explanation="Most systems have a threshold below which effort produces negligible results and above which momentum becomes self-sustaining. The growth curve looks flat, then vertical. The hard work isn't scaling — it's surviving long enough to reach the threshold."
  relevance="Building habits, growing audiences, startup traction, network effects, social movements, any domain where compounding only kicks in after a certain density."
>}}

{{< principle
  name="Inversion"
  tagline="Figure out what you want to avoid, then don't do that."
  explanation="Instead of asking 'how do I succeed?', ask 'what would guarantee failure?' Mapping the failure modes is often clearer and more actionable than imagining success. Avoiding the obvious pitfalls eliminates most of the variance; success tends to follow."
  relevance="Problem-solving, strategy, risk management, habit design, any situation where the path forward is unclear but the paths to failure are visible."
>}}
