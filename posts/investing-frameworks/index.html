Investing Frameworks

A living collection of mental models I use to evaluate businesses and time positions.
May 25, 2026 · map[email:shrijit@umich.edu name:Shrijit Patel]

A running list of frameworks I lean on when sizing up a business — collected here so I can grow it over time. Click any card for the full one-pager.

The Two Engine Framework Stock prices are driven by two engines — EPS and PE. Multi-baggers need both.
▼

A stock's return decomposes into two engines: the EPS engine (fundamental earnings growth) and the PE engine (multiple expansion driven by sentiment, moat strength, and margin trajectory). Linear returns come when only EPS expands. Exponential returns come when both engines fire together. The same company can make one investor 4x and lose another money over a decade — the difference is whether the PE engine still has runway at entry.

Use it when: Position sizing, entry timing, distinguishing 'good business at a fair price' from 'good business at a finished price.'

The Margin Framework Profitability lives in layers — and only some of them compound.
▼

A business's margin profile breaks into three layers. Layer 1 is core operating leverage (gross/operating margins that scale with revenue). Layer 2 is pricing power and value-added services (premium pricing, high-margin upsells — think Mastercard, Apple). Layer 3 is asset-light scalability — 'floating DNA' businesses that grow revenue globally without proportional capex, debt, or dilution. The deeper a business reaches into Layer 3, the longer its compounding runway.

Use it when: Diagnosing business quality, separating durable compounders from one-time growth stories, predicting which margins will expand vs. mean-revert.
